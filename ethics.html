Ethics

Computer ethics is concerned with how computing professionals should make decisions and use their computers in ethically desirable ways. Here are ten commandments of Computer Ethics. The commandments were created in 1992 by the Computer Ethics Institute, a nonprofit research, education, and public policy organization, with the archaic style of the Ten Commandments from the King James Bible.

1. Thou shalt not use a computer to harm other people.
2. Thou shalt not interfere with other people's computer work.
3. Thou shalt not snoop around in other people's computer files.
4. Thou shalt not use a computer to steal.
5. Thou shalt not use a computer to bear false witness.
6. Thou shalt not copy or use proprietary software for which you have not paid.
7. Thou shalt not use other people's computer resources without authorization or proper compensation.
8. Thou shalt not appropriate other people's intellectual output.
9. Thou shalt think about the social consequences of the program you are writing or the system you are designing.
10. Thou shalt always use a computer in ways that ensure consideration and respect for your fellow humans.
